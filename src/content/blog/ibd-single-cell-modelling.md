In 2024 we published [Longitudinal single-cell data informs deterministic modelling of inflammatory bowel disease](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41540-024-00395-9) in *npj Systems Biology and Applications* (Kilian, Ulrich, Zouboulis, et al.).

The core idea is to let high-resolution, longitudinal single-cell measurements constrain a deterministic model of IBD — so the model is not only mathematically tidy, but grounded in how cell states actually change over time.

I care about this bridge between **omics-scale observation** and **mechanistic modelling**: it is one route toward tools that are both data-rich and clinically interpretable. More notes on methods and follow-ups may appear here later.
